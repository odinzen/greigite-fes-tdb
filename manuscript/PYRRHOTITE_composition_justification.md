# Why the equilibrium pyrrhotite is troilite-end FeS₁.₀₀–FeS₁.₁₄ (not FeS₁.₀₉₂ or Fe₀.₈₇₅S)

**The question:** justify, with references, the statement that the free-energy-minimized
pyrrhotite composition lies at the troilite end.

## The composition is not imposed — it is the free-energy minimum of a sublattice model
Pyrrhotite in the database is the non-stoichiometric **(Fe,Va)₁(S)₁** compound-energy-formalism
phase of the Dilner–Mao–Selleby (2015) assessment, whose Fe–S edge derives from Lee, Sundman,
Kim & Chin (ISIJ). Iron/vacancy mixing on the metal sublattice makes the equilibrium Fe₁₋ₓS
composition a **function of sulfur fugacity and temperature**, solved by Gibbs-energy
minimization — it is an internal variable, not an assumption. Across the low-temperature
(300–600 K), low-f(S₂) conditions that bound greigite's stability field, the minimizing
composition sits at the **Fe-rich (troilite) end, X(S) = 0.500–0.53 (FeS₁.₀₀–FeS₁.₁₄),
widening with T** (engine, this work).

## This is consistent with the experimental Fe–S system
- **Stoichiometric troilite (FeS)** is the stable low-temperature monosulfide, below the
  troilite → hexagonal-pyrrhotite transition near ~415 K (Grønvold & Stølen 1992;
  Wang & Salveson 2005).
- The more **Fe-deficient superstructures** — monoclinic 4C **Fe₇S₈ (Fe₀.₈₇₅S)** and the
  NC/NA hexagonal pyrrhotites — are stable only at **higher f(S₂) and higher temperature**
  (Nakazawa & Morimoto 1971; Wang & Salveson 2005).
- The pyrrhotite composition is a well-established **monotonic function of f(S₂)** at fixed T
  (Toulmin & Barton 1964).

## Conclusion
The commonly-quoted **FeS₁.₀₉₂ (≈Fe₀.₉₂S)** and **Fe₀.₈₇₅S** are specific compositions
appropriate to higher-f(S₂)/T conditions; they are **not** the equilibrium composition
coexisting with iron and greigite at low temperature. We therefore (i) report pyrrhotite at its
**free, energy-minimized composition** (troilite-end, citing Lee/Dilner), and (ii) separately
identify the **observed thermal-decomposition product, pyrrhotite-3T Fe₇S₈ (FeS₁.₁₄)**, as a
kinetic composition — the two are distinct quantities and are labelled as such on every figure.
